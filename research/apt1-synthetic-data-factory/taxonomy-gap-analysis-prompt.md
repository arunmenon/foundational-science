# Taxonomy Gap-Analysis Prompt (external cross-check)

**Purpose:** Break the circularity flagged in [02 §4A](02-ppa-and-taxonomy.md) by validating our *generated* fraud/commerce taxonomy against **independent, published** fraud typologies. Feed this prompt + the full contents of `02-ppa-and-taxonomy.md` to a strong LLM (ideally a different model family than the one that generated the taxonomy, for independence).

**How to use:** paste everything below the line into the LLM, then paste the taxonomy where indicated.

---

You are a senior fraud & financial-crime taxonomy expert (payments, scams/APP fraud, AML/CFT, card fraud, account-takeover, merchant risk). Your job is a rigorous, adversarial **gap analysis** of a fraud/commerce taxonomy that was generated by an AI from public sources, so it may have blind spots. Do NOT assume it is complete. Be exhaustive and skeptical.

## Inputs
1. OUR TAXONOMY (the artifact to critique) is pasted at the end under `=== OUR TAXONOMY ===`.
2. Use as INDEPENDENT reference frameworks (triangulate against these; do not rely only on your own opinion):
   - ACFE Fraud Tree (occupational/asset-misappropriation/financial-statement categories where relevant)
   - FATF / FinCEN AML-CFT typologies (placement/layering/integration, mule networks, structuring, trade-based ML)
   - UK PSR / industry **APP (authorized push payment) scam categories** (investment, romance, impersonation/CEO, invoice/BEC, purchase, advance-fee, safe-account)
   - Card-scheme **fraud reason codes / chargeback reason codes** (Visa/Mastercard categories)
   - Standard account-fraud vectors: account takeover (credential stuffing, SIM-swap, phishing, session hijack, MFA-bypass), synthetic identity, new-account fraud, first-party/"friendly" fraud, promo/bonus abuse, refund/return abuse, reseller/triangulation fraud, merchant bust-out & collusion, chargeback fraud, loyalty-points fraud
   - Payments-specific: money mule recruitment, P2P scams, crypto off-ramp scams, gift-card scams, payment-method/instrument fraud, BIN attacks/card testing

## What to do
1. **Map**: for each independent reference category above, find where (if anywhere) it appears in OUR TAXONOMY. Quote the exact node name from our taxonomy when it maps.
2. **Find gaps**: list every reference category (or important real-world vector) that is **missing, only partially covered, or mis-scoped** in our taxonomy.
3. **Find structural problems**: flag nodes in our taxonomy that are **redundant, overlapping, mislabeled, wrongly nested, or wrongly tagged** CX (customer-facing) vs internal.
4. **Adversarial sweep**: think like a fraudster — name attack vectors / scam types a real program must handle that our taxonomy does not represent.
5. **Action-space implication**: for each proposed new entry, note what **agent actions/tools** a customer-facing agent would need to handle it (so it can feed the action-space coverage check).

## Output format (use these sections exactly)
### A. Coverage map
A table: `Reference category | Mapped to our node? (quote, or "MISSING/PARTIAL") | Notes`.

### B. Proposed additions (the gaps)
For each, a row/block with:
- `name` — concise proposed node name
- `parent` — where it belongs in our tree (domain → subdomain → sub-bucket)
- `definition` — 1-2 sentences
- `maps_to` — which external framework/category it comes from (the independence anchor)
- `cx_or_internal` — CX | INT | CX/INT
- `why_it_matters` — fraud/loss impact + why omission is risky
- `example_scenario` — one concrete case
- `needed_agent_actions` — tools/actions an agent would require (for action-space coverage)
- `confidence` — high | medium | low
- `priority` — high | medium | low

### C. Structural fixes
List redundant/overlapping/mislabeled/mis-nested/mis-tagged nodes with the suggested fix.

### D. Verdict
- Overall coverage assessment (how representative is our taxonomy vs the external frameworks?).
- The 5-10 **highest-priority** missing entries.
- An explicit list of **"standard categories we are clearly missing" (high confidence)** vs **"edge/speculative"** — keep these separate.

## Rules
- Ground every proposed gap in a NAMED external framework or a concrete real-world fraud pattern — not vague intuition.
- Do NOT claim something is present in our taxonomy unless you can quote the node; if unsure, mark MISSING/PARTIAL.
- Do NOT invent statistics. Flag where you are uncertain.
- Prefer being over-inclusive in section B (it is a candidate list for human review, not a final decision).
