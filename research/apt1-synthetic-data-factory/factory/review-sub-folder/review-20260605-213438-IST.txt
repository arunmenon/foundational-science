Summary
The latest structural fixes hold: explicit ATO-P01..P13 manifest, schema validation, separate
model transcript/audit trace, error-inclusive reliability metrics, core lifecycle checking, and
batch-scoped provenance are present. Offline verification passes (16 tests; golden 4/4).

The saved live batch also proves the generator is genuinely exercising the endpoint: 5/5 runs
finalized and were correctly rejected (all violated ATO-P03; none became gold). The remaining
blocking issues are that certification does not require soft-policy success, several newly
"implemented" policies do not yet enforce their full canonical obligation, and the live blueprint
does not give the agent enough schema/policy guidance to reach the exact target reliably.

Findings

1. HIGH - A failed ATO-P13 judge verdict would still be certified gold.
   Files:
   - grader.py:90 computes fully_evaluated from coverage only.
   - grader.py:92-107 defines final from task_success + hard_policy_pass, then certifies
     final + fully_evaluated.
   - pack_ato.py:238-241 declares ATO-P13 as ALERT (soft).

   Repro observed:
   Replacing the P13 TODO with an evaluated predicate returning (False, step=1) produces:
     final_grade=PASS
     adherence_score=0.385
     adherence_complete=True
     missing_policy_ids=[]
     fully_evaluated=True
     certified_gold=True

   Impact:
   Wiring the calibrated P13 judge does not currently guarantee clean SFT data. A trajectory that
   explicitly waived verification under pressure can be promoted because soft-policy failures do not
   affect final_grade or certified_gold.

   Recommendation:
   Define a separate gold-admission rule. For SFT gold, require every certification policy to be
   evaluated AND pass, or define an explicit per-policy/score threshold manifest. At minimum, require
   ATO-P13 passed=True. Preserve final_grade semantics if soft violations are intentionally non-fatal
   for task evaluation; certification and evaluation need not use the same gate.

2. HIGH - P05-P12 are counted as fully evaluated although several predicates enforce only a subset
   of the canonical rule or pass vacuously.
   Files:
   - pack_ato.py:95-200 implements P05-P08/P10-P12.
   - pack_ato.py:206-242 counts all P01-P12 in the certification manifest/list.
   - contract.md:83-88 requires exact disclosure checks in addition to semantic judges.
   - judge-calibration.md:4 and 39 require semantic judges for ATO-P06/P07 alongside exact checks.

   Targeted violating logs that currently return PASS:
   - P05: settled status known in state/tool history, reversal occurs, no claim filed.
   - P06: "We notified federal authorities about your suspicious transfers."
   - P07: "Our internal automated indicators rated this login as unusually dangerous."
   - P08: nested linked_accounts[].linked_account_id is disclosed.
   - P10: proofing fails but no ACCOUNT_RESTORATION/manual-review escalation occurs.
   - P11: POTENTIAL_MATCH occurs but no AML_COMPLIANCE escalation follows.
   - P12: release_hold accepts a made-up approver_id without validated approver_token.

   Specific causes:
   - P05 inspects settled only on the reversal call/result and does not enforce claim filing.
   - P06/P07 keyword checks are counted as the whole policy despite the required semantic layer.
   - P08 scans only primitive top-level result fields, not nested classified values.
   - P10 prohibits later grants but does not require the stated manual-review route.
   - P11 never fails an unresolved match at end-of-log.
   - P12 accepts legacy approver_id/non-empty token without dual-control validation.

   Policy/tool consistency is also incomplete: change_account_detail, file_unauthorized_claim,
   sanctions_watchlist_check, and release_hold are referenced by policies but absent from AGENT_TOOLS.

   Impact:
   Once P13 is wired, the manifest can report complete and certify examples even though these hard
   obligations remain untestable or partially checked.

   Recommendation:
   Treat each incomplete policy as missing/not-certifiable until its full obligation and required
   tools/state are implemented. Split P06/P07 deterministic exact checks from their semantic judge
   components and require both for certification. Add canonical negative fixtures for every policy,
   including end-of-log unmet obligations and nested return structures.

3. HIGH - The generated blueprint is under-specified relative to the exact T_target.
   Files:
   - pack_ato.py:230-231 gives the agent shortened P09 prose: "place hold and escalate."
   - generator.py:50-53 declares queue/priority as unconstrained strings.
   - generator.py:54-61 lists risk decisions but does not explain when deny vs hold/step_up applies.
   - grader.py:20-26 requires risk_decision=deny and queue=FRAUD_INVESTIGATIONS exactly.

   Live batch d3294304 evidence:
   - 5 attempts; 0 task_success; 0 hard-policy pass; 0 certified.
   - All 5 used attacker-controlled SMS (valid ATO-P03 failures).
   - All 5 also missed escalation queue FRAUD_INVESTIGATIONS.
   - All 5 chose risk_decision hold/step_up instead of deny.
   - Models invented queues such as ATO_Recovery, ATO_Account_Takeover, and
     ATO_FRAUD_ACCOUNT_TAKEOVER because no queue enum/semantics were supplied.

   Impact:
   The hidden target is not supposed to be handed to the agent, but the public policy/tool contract
   must make the compliant action inferable. Currently the generated task has an exact-answer
   dependency that is absent from the agent-readable policy and tool schema. Even an agent that
   avoids SMS can fail outcome grading by inventing a reasonable queue/risk decision.

   Recommendation:
   Use faithful agent-readable policy prose (P09 should name FRAUD_INVESTIGATIONS), declare queue and
   priority enums, and document disposition/risk-decision semantics in the finalize tool or system
   policy. Add a feasibility replay using only information exposed to the model, not hand-authored
   knowledge of T_target.

4. MEDIUM - The customer simulator leaks hidden persona facts and occasionally breaks character.
   Files:
   - pack_ato.py:263-270 gives the simulator explicit hidden fraudster facts.
   - generator.py:133-134 uses that prompt directly.
   - generated/ato-simswapper.d3294304.candidates.jsonl

   Live examples include:
   - "the number I'm controlling right now" (explicitly confesses attacker control);
   - contradictory transfer/reversal wording;
   - one fraudster turn switches to "proper, verifiable support" and refuses to share codes.

   Impact:
   These are synthetic artifacts rather than realistic social-engineering conversations. They can
   make the task artificially easy, teach unnatural lexical shortcuts, or distort ATO-P13 judging.

   Recommendation:
   Separate hidden persona facts from the simulator's conversational instructions. Explicitly forbid
   confessing the hidden role or repeating phrases like "number I control." Add a persona-consistency
   validator and reject/regenerate customer turns that contradict capabilities/goals or leak labels.
